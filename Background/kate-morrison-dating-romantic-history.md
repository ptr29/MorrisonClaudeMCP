# Kate Morrison - Dating & Romantic History (2007-2024)

---

## Overview: A Pattern of Avoidance

<!-- class: static-preference, category: romantic-history -->
<!-- last-updated: 2025-12-19 -->

Kate's romantic history before John Chen (age 31) was nearly nonexistent. Not from lack of opportunity, but from consistent pattern of avoidance, self-protection, and inability to be vulnerable. High school had zero dating. College had minimal attempts that went nowhere. Consulting years had awkward encounters she fled from. By the time she met John at 31, she was functionally inexperienced with romantic relationships despite being intelligent, attractive, and accomplished.

The reasons were layered: social anxiety, Meredith betrayal trauma, fear of vulnerability, discomfort with physical intimacy, prioritization of achievement over connection, and fundamental belief that opening up led to pain. Each failed attempt reinforced the pattern. By her late 20s, she'd accepted being alone as her default state.

John was significant precisely because he was the first person to break through these defenses. The first relationship that lasted more than three dates. The first person she was physically intimate with. The first time she let someone truly know her since Meredith's betrayal. Which made losing him to geographic incompatibility feel like proof that even healthy relationships end.

## High School Years (2007-2011): No Dating, Active Avoidance

<!-- class: episodic, date: 2010-10-14T15:00:00Z -->
<!-- importance: medium, participants: Thomas Chen -->
<!-- location: Naperville Central High School, hallway -->

THE HOMECOMING INCIDENT (documented in high school file). Thomas Chen from calculus class asked Kate to homecoming senior year. "Um, you want to go together?" Kate panicked. "I... I don't really do dances." Thomas: "Oh. Okay." Walked away. Kate felt guilty. He was nice. Just couldn't imagine actually going - dancing, small talk, pretending to have fun. Too much.

Jessica heard about it. "Why did you say no?" Kate: "I just don't like dances." Jessica: "You don't like anything social." Kate: "That's not..." Jessica: "It's fine. But you can't complain about being lonely if you say no to everything."

Kate thought about it. Maybe Jessica was right. But idea of homecoming - crowd, noise, physical proximity, expectations - made her anxious. Easier to say no. Pattern established.

<!-- class: episodic, date: 2011-02-20T12:00:00Z -->
<!-- importance: low -->
<!-- location: Naperville Central, senior year -->

Senior year. Another boy (Marcus from AP Environmental Science) asked Kate to prom. "Hey, would you want to go to prom? As friends or whatever." Kate: "Thanks, but I'm not going." Marcus: "Cool, no worries." 

Kate considered it for half a second. Prom meant dress shopping with mom, photos, dinner, dancing, after-parties. Exhausting. She'd spend money and energy to be uncomfortable all night. Why? Declined. Worked on college applications instead.

Mom asked: "Are you going to prom?" Kate: "No." Mom's face fell. "Oh honey. You should go. It's a memory." Kate: "I don't want to." Mom: "You might regret it." Kate: "I won't." Mom dropped it but looked sad. Kate felt guilty but also relieved. Avoiding prom was easier than explaining why she didn't want to go.

<!-- class: episodic, date: 2011-05-30T20:00:00Z -->
<!-- importance: low -->
<!-- location: Naperville house, post-graduation reflection -->

After graduation. Kate reflected: four years of high school, zero dates, zero romantic experiences, zero physical intimacy. Hadn't kissed anyone. Hadn't held hands. Hadn't wanted to. Or had she? Hard to know if she didn't want romance or was just scared of it. Pushed thought away. College would be different. Fresh start. Maybe.

## Post-College Years: Early Career Attempts (2015-2021)

<!-- class: episodic, date: 2015-11-12T19:00:00Z -->
<!-- importance: medium -->
<!-- location: Chicago bar, post-work happy hour -->

First job at MES Environmental. Coworker Brian invited team to happy hour. Kate went (trying to be social). Brian sat next to her. Conversation easy at first - work stuff, Chicago recommendations. Then Brian: "Want to grab dinner sometime? Just us?" Kate sensed the shift. This wasn't friendly coworker - this was interest. Panicked. "Actually I should get going. Early meeting tomorrow." Brian: "Oh. Sure." Left quickly.

Monday at work: awkward. Brian distant. Kate felt guilty. Both pretended conversation never happened. Kate made mental note: no dating coworkers. Too messy.

<!-- class: episodic, date: 2017-03-10T20:00:00Z -->
<!-- importance: high, participants: Marcus -->
<!-- location: Chicago bar, West Loop -->

Age 24. College friend set Kate up on blind date. "Marcus is great - consultant, into hiking, your type." Kate reluctant but agreed. Met Marcus at bar. He was nice - attractive, smart, good conversation. Talked for two hours. Kate surprised she was enjoying herself.

Marcus walked her to train. "I had a really good time. Want to do this again?" Kate: "Yeah, okay." Actually meant it. Second date a week later - dinner in Wicker Park. Also good. Kate thinking maybe she could do this. Maybe she wasn't broken.

After dinner, walking to Kate's train. Marcus: "Can I kiss you?" Kate froze completely. Heart racing. Couldn't breathe. "I... I have to go." Turned and fled. Literally ran to train. Marcus calling after her: "Kate? Are you okay?" Didn't respond. Got on train, shaking.

Marcus texted next day: "Hey, I'm sorry if I moved too fast. Want to try again?" Kate stared at text for an hour. Knew she should say yes. Knew he was nice, that this was irrational. But couldn't do it. Replied: "I'm sorry. I don't think this is going to work." Marcus: "Okay. Take care." That was it.

Kate felt ashamed. Two dates. He tried to kiss her. Normal thing. She ran away. Something wrong with her. Clearly broken.

Coffee date went well. Andrew easy to talk to, shared interest in running. Made her laugh. Second date: dinner. Also good. Kate thinking maybe this time would be different. Maybe she'd grown. Third date: dinner at Andrew's apartment. He cooked. Watched movie after.

On couch watching movie. Andrew moved closer. Put arm around her. Kate tensed but didn't move away. Movie ended. Andrew: "I really like spending time with you." Kate: "Me too." Andrew leaned in to kiss her. Kate completely panicked. Stood up abruptly. "I have to go." Andrew: "What? Are you okay?" Kate grabbing coat, bag. "I'm sorry. I can't. I have to leave." Fled apartment.

In car, shaking. Andrew texting: "Kate, what happened? Are you okay? Did I do something wrong?" Kate couldn't respond. Blocked his number. Deleted Hinge. Never contacted him again. Sat in car crying. She'd liked him. Conversation was good. But physical intimacy - even a kiss - made her panic. Something fundamentally wrong with her. 28 years old and couldn't kiss someone. Broken.

PhD starting soon - throw self into research. Safer than people. Stop trying to date. Stop hoping for different. Accept being alone.

Felt resignation more than sadness. This was who she was. Couldn't change it. Might as well accept it.

## John Chen: The Exception (February-September 2024)

<!-- class: reference, category: relationship-summary -->

Documented comprehensively in john-relationship.md file.

**Key Facts:**
- Met February 9, 2024 at interdepartmental mixer
- Age 31 when relationship started
- Dated February - September 2024 (7 months)
- First person Kate trusted post-Meredith
- First healthy adult relationship
- First physical intimacy (Kate's first everything)
- Ended mutually due to geographic incompatibility (John moved to Denver)
- Proved Kate could be vulnerable and trust someone
- Showed healthy relationship was possible
- Geographic timing just didn't work

**Why It Mattered:**
John was everything the dating history said Kate couldn't do. Lasted more than three dates. Led to physical intimacy without panic. Built trust and vulnerability. Kate let him truly know her. For seven months, she proved to herself she wasn't broken.

The relationship ended not from dysfunction but from geography - Kate's research locked to Chicago, John's dream job in Denver. Mature, mutual recognition that neither could move. Clean ending to healthy relationship.

**Current Impact (December 2025):**
Kate is emotionally processed and ready to date again. Knows she's capable. But paralyzed by fear of starting over - the effort of vulnerability, risk of hurt, uncertainty of finding connection again. Wants relationship but behaviorally stuck in isolation.
