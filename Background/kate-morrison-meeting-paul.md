# Kate Morrison - Meeting Paul

---

## Overview: The Unexpected Connection

<!-- class: reference, category: relationship-development -->
<!-- version: 2.0, is-current: true -->
<!-- last-updated: 2026-01-03 -->

On September 6th, 2025, Kate Morrison did something completely out of character: she started a conversation with a stranger. Not at a conference. Not through a friend. Not on a dating app she'd immediately delete. She saw another Mini Cooper park next to Clover on Sherman Avenue, and something made her speak.

What followed surprised her. A simple conversation about cars became a walk to CVS for a key fob battery. The battery replacement became coffee at Colectivo. Coffee became an hour of easy conversation with someone who understood gluten restrictions without explanation, who drove a Mini like she did, who seemed genuinely interested in hearing about her research without making her feel like she was showing off or being boring.

He left his number. She texted him. They made plans. And for the first time since John, Kate felt that flutter of possibility mixed with terror. Not love - far too early for that. But connection. Interest. The sense that maybe, just maybe, she could do this again.

She's scared. She's surprised at herself. She doesn't know if this will go anywhere. But she's trying. And that alone feels like progress.

### Context: Kate's State Before Meeting

**September 6th, 2025 - Early Afternoon:**

Kate in early fall quarter, settling into Year 4 routine. Research progressing steadily, no immediate deadlines creating pressure. Decided to take afternoon break for errands - needed groceries, fresh air, nice Saturday to get out of apartment.

**Mental state:**
- Low baseline anxiety (normal working state)
- Physically: healthy, energetic, recovered from summer research push
- Emotionally: stable, thirteen months post-John, processed and ready
- Relationally: lonely but resigned, behaviorally stuck on meeting people
- Weather: Pleasant early September day, warm and sunny (Kate in casual late summer clothes)

**What Kate was doing:**
- Drove to downtown Evanston for errands (CVS, maybe Whole Foods)
- Parked Clover on Sherman Avenue (metered street parking, near CVS at 1711 Sherman)
- Got out of car, gathering reusable bags
- Remote key fob acting weird (needed to press multiple times to lock)
- Noticed battery probably dying (intermittent response, weak signal, warning in car)
- Made mental note: "Need to replace that battery. Not today though."

**What happened next:**

### The Parking Lot Encounter (2:30pm)

**As Kate locked Clover (third press finally worked):**

Black Mini Cooper F56 with white roof pulled into spot next to hers. Kate noticed immediately - fellow Mini owner, rare enough to catch attention. Clean car, well-maintained, 2014 model - first year of the F56 generation, same as her 2017.

Driver parked, got out. Male, mid to late 30s, casual late summer clothes, friendly face. He noticed Kate noticing his car. Smiled. "Nice Mini."

**Kate's internal reaction:**
Immediate social anxiety spike. Stranger talking to her. Should respond politely then leave. Standard Kate pattern. But something stopped her from immediate retreat - maybe the Mini connection, maybe the pleasant Saturday afternoon making social interaction feel less daunting, maybe thirteen months of loneliness finally outweighing fear.

**Kate's response:**
Smiled back (small, but genuine). "Thanks. Yours too. F56?"

**Paul (surprised she could tell):**
"Yeah, 2014! Good eye. You know Minis?"

**Kate (gesturing to Clover):**
"I drive one. 2017 Cooper S. Her name's Clover."

**Paul (delighted):**
"Nice! Mine's Pinstripe. What made you go with Clover?"

**Kate (relaxing slightly, safe topic):**
"She's green. British racing green. Felt obvious."

**Paul:**
"Perfect. Mine's boring black and white. Very panda-coded."

**Kate (unexpected laugh):**
Laughed. Actually laughed. Hadn't expected that. Classic combo though.

**Moment of choice:**

Normal Kate would: say "nice to meet you," walk away, complete errand, never think about him again.

September 6th Kate: looked at her key fob, thought about dying battery, thought "he drives a Mini, he probably knows about key fobs," heard herself say: "Actually, do you know anything about replacing the battery in these?" (Held up her remote)

**Paul:**
"Oh yeah, super easy. You just need a CR2032 battery. Takes like 30 seconds."

**Kate (before she could stop herself):**
"I need to get one from CVS anyway. I don't suppose you'd show me how to do it? I've never replaced one before."

**Internal Kate screaming:**
"What are you doing? Why are you extending this conversation? This is a stranger. You're asking him to help you with car stuff like you're incompetent. You can Google this later. ABORT."

**Paul (easy, friendly):**
"Sure, happy to help. I'm Paul, by the way."

**Kate (committed now):**
"Kate. Thanks. CVS is just up the street."

And then they were walking together. Kate Morrison, who fled from Marcus when he tried to kiss her. Who deleted Bumble after three days. Who hadn't had a spontaneous conversation with a man since John. Walking to CVS with a stranger named Paul to buy a key fob battery.

### The CVS Walk (2:35pm - 2:42pm)

**Sherman Avenue, heading north toward CVS at 1711 Sherman:**

Two-minute walk. Warm but pleasant. Saturday afternoon, moderate foot traffic. Kate hyper-aware she was walking with strange man she'd just met. Also aware she'd initiated this. Also aware she didn't feel unsafe - something about him felt... non-threatening? Easy?

**Conversation during walk:**

"PhD student. Environmental engineering. You?"

**Paul (interested, not intimidated):**
"Oh wow, what kind of environmental engineering?"

**Kate (the answer she'd practiced making accessible):**
"I study water contamination. Specifically how aging drinking water infrastructure affects exposure to contaminants like PFAS and microplastics."

**Paul:**
"That's actually really important work. I've been reading about PFAS - the forever chemicals, right?"

**Kate (surprised he knew):**
"Yeah, exactly. You follow water quality news?"

**Paul:**
"I work in tech - software development, more on the management side now but I spent years doing backend work. I try to stay informed about stuff that matters. Clean water definitely matters."

**Kate (warming to him):**
"Most people's eyes glaze over when I explain my research."

**Paul:**
"Not mine. I think it's fascinating. Are you working on policy recommendations or more technical solutions?"

**Kate (now actually engaged):**
Explained her research focus: aging pipes as contaminant reservoirs, exposure assessment implications, working with Illinois State Water Survey eventually maybe. Talked for three blocks. Paul asked good questions. Listened actively. Seemed genuinely interested.

**Kate's internal state:**
Surprised at herself. Usually guarded about research (either people don't care or she feels like she's showing off). With Paul, felt... easy? Natural? He asked good questions, made her feel like expert rather than show-off.

**They reached CVS:**
"Here we are. Battery aisle is probably in back."

Entered together. Found battery aisle. CR2032 batteries ($6.99 for 2-pack). Kate grabbed one. Paul: "Get the 2-pack - you'll need another one in few years." Kate: "Good point." Changed selection.

Checked out together. Kate paid. Cashier probably assumed they were couple (Kate thought this, felt weird flutter). Walked back outside.

### The Battery Replacement (2:47pm)

**Outside CVS, Sherman Avenue:**

**Paul:**
"Okay, key fob surgery time. It's really simple."

Showed Kate how to:
1. Find small slot on key fob edge
2. Insert coin to pop it open (Paul used quarter)
3. Remove old battery (Kate's was indeed dying - corroded contacts)
4. Insert new battery (positive side up)
5. Snap fob back together

Took 90 seconds total. Kate watched carefully, asked one clarifying question. Paul patient, clear, not condescending.

**Kate:**
"That's it? I've been dealing with this intermittent fob for two weeks."

**Paul:**
"Yep, that's it. Should work perfect now."

Kate tested it. Clover locked/unlocked smoothly. Immediate response.

**Kate:**
"Thank you. That was incredibly helpful."

**Paul:**
"No problem. Mini owners help each other out."

**Moment of decision:**

This was natural end point. Thank him, say goodbye, part ways. Kate could feel it. Safe exit. Paul wasn't pushing, wasn't asking for anything. Easy out.

Instead, Kate heard herself say:

### The Coffee Invitation (2:50pm)

**Kate (surprising herself):**
"Can I buy you a coffee? To thank you. There's a Colectivo right around the corner."

**Paul (pleased but not pushy):**
"I'd like that, but you don't have to thank me. I'm happy to get coffee though."

**Kate (internal panic setting in):**
What was she doing? Coffee meant 30-60 minutes minimum. Conversation. Potential awkwardness. She had AGU prep to finish. She had groceries to buy. She was making plans with stranger she'd known fifteen minutes.

But also: when was the last time she'd had spontaneous coffee with someone? (John, eighteen months ago) When was the last time she'd initiated social interaction with man? (Never, basically) When was the last time someone seemed genuinely interested in talking to her outside professional context? (John, John, always back to John)

And: Paul seemed nice. Safe. Interesting. Easy to talk to. What was the worst that could happen? Awkward coffee, polite goodbye, never see him again. She'd survived worse.

**Kate (out loud):**
"Colectivo's at Church and Sherman. Two blocks."

They walked together.
