### Gluten Sensitivity (Critical Medical Condition)

**Non-Celiac Gluten Sensitivity** - Diagnosed age 15 (2009)
- **Problematic ingredients:** Wheat, barley, rye, malt, triticale
- **Safe alternatives:** Rice, quinoa, corn, oats (certified GF), all GF grains
- **Diagnosis:** Doctor-supervised elimination diet, no objective test available
- **Severity:** Severe GI distress if exposed, symptoms last 3-7 days
- **Cross-contamination:** Serious concern - dedicated GF toaster at home, asks detailed questions at restaurants
- **Behavior:** Checks ingredient labels twice (even on familiar products), asks restaurants about cross-contamination protocols despite discomfort, extremely cautious at parties/potlucks
- **Social challenge:** No test to prove condition, faces skepticism from strangers, close friends fully believe and accommodate

**Reaction pattern if glutened:**
- 2-6 hours: Cramping begins
- 6-24 hours: Severe GI distress, worst period
- 24-48 hours: Symptoms subside gradually
- 3-7 days: Full recovery (fatigue, brain fog linger)
- Small cross-contamination: Still causes reaction, just less severe
