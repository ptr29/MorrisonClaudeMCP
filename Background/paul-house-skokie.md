## Overview

**Property Type:** Mid-century Chicago-area brick home  
**Location:** Skokie, Illinois (Chicago suburbs)  
**Structure:** Two-level residence (main level + fully built-out lower level)  
**Design Philosophy:** Defined rooms rather than open plan, generous circulation space, designed for long-term family living and flexible use  
**Footprint:** Wider than deep, providing many rooms with exterior walls and windows

**Overall Character:** A solid, practical home emphasizing privacy, acoustic separation, and distinct functional zones. Not trend-driven or minimalist - built for comfortable, adaptable living with unusually large basement living areas.

## Functional Analysis

### Strengths
1. **Excellent bedroom separation** - Main level bedrooms positioned away from public spaces
2. **Large, usable basement living areas** - Recreation room is exceptional in size
3. **Multiple bathrooms on both levels** - Dual-bath main level plus full bath in basement
4. **Defined rooms for privacy and acoustics** - Traditional hallway layouts
5. **Strong balance between public and private space**
6. **Generous circulation space** - Wide hallways and proper transitional areas
7. **Multi-use flexibility** - Lower level dramatically increases livable square footage

### Design Philosophy
- Emphasizes **defined rooms over open concept**
- Prioritizes **privacy and acoustic separation**
- Built for **long-term family living** and **flexible entertaining**
- **Not minimalist** - practical and adaptable spaces
- **Multi-generational capable** - independent lower level with full amenities

### Overall Feel
Practical, solid, and adaptable. Well-suited to:
- Families
- Multi-generational living
- Owners who value distinct rooms over open-concept layouts
- Those who want finished basement space for recreation/guests

**Size Perception:** House feels much larger than it appears from exterior due to fully developed lower level.
